Do I need to tell my children about the family budget?

Hello, dear readers.

So the first academic week of this year at the school has ended.

My son went away for a two-week vacation to the country. We didn't do anything about financial literacy either.

But this week we're going to we are entering the school schedule with new forces, and we continue to study the topic of financial literacy.

I noticed that in the only two weeks I had been studying with him, he had begun to show some interest. He started asking questions, especially about investments. And very much waiting for when he can make your first investment.

This week, we started reading the book "money, or the Alphabet of money"with him. He likes the book very much, and is already looking forward to reading the sequel.

In the book, there was a task for the girl Kira to write 10 points why it is good to be rich. We my son and I also wrote 10 points each.

I will not list them, but my son, like many other children, has some toys and Hobbies included in this list. And everything connected with it.

In addition, during the two weeks of my son's absence from home, I devoted a lot of time to studying the topic of "financial literacy for all children's".

I made a lot of notes for myself. One of them is that it is possible to tell children about the family budget somewhere from the age of 3-4 years. There is no, of course, not directly sit and explain.

But in the course of our actions, tell our children where we get our money, clothes, and toys. And that we can't all the money spend it on entertainment.

In addition, the child should be initiated into financial planning of the family budget. Naturally, to do this, you need to start keeping a budget!

Therefore, this week my son and I talked about the financial budget.

And not just talking. He was very young. task to create a budget for the next month. He had managed the task well, and had forgotten only two items. But otherwise, he remembered all our expenses, mandatory and not so much.

In addition, this week he received 200 rubles for pocket expenses+ change from change. His task was to buy bread on the way home from school. Therefore, if I give money more than the cost of bread, he keeps the change for himself.

He had spent the change. And pocket money, as always, spread out in their piggy banks: 100 R for investments, 50 R for animatronics and another 50 R in another piggy Bank without a name yet.

The piggy Bank for animatronics is saved up from the very beginning, because in the last day of the vacation, her son reset it by buying an animatronica.

Do you devote your children to financial planning of the family budget? Write in the comments.
