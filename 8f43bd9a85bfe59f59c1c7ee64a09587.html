What stocks does Larisa Morozova have in her portfolio

Larisa Morozova is a private Russian investor. She started investing in stocks in 2008 at the age of 53.

For 11 years, she has studied the Russian stock market, lives on dividends and teaches this to other people.

Unfortunately, Larisa does not disclose the structure and volume of her portfolio, and her favorite saying is "Money loves silence". But this does not mean that it does not share information about its interests stocks.

She does not hide what stocks she is interested in and what she pays attention to.

First of all, Larisa believes that the most important thing to pay attention to in shares is the majority shareholders. Who they are and whether they are interested in receiving dividends.

Majority shareholders are large owners of shares, the size of which allows making decisions on the company's management.

It is best that these majoritaries are the government of the Russian Federation, ministries of the Russian Federation, subjects of the Russian Federation, Rosimushchestvo, and so on, i.e. those whose dividends are budgeted.

Why is this important? In the budget of our country and regions initially, there are dividends that go to various social programs, salaries, and so on.

And in this case, if the company wants to refuse or reduce the payment of dividends, the majority shareholders will insist on receiving their profit, because it is budgeted.

Besides it majoritaries may also be other companies that are interested in receiving dividends in order to pay, for example, a loan or cover some production costs.

This means that minority shareholders, i.e. ordinary private investors, will also receive their share.

Therefore Larisa thoroughly studies each of them the company and finds out who is majoritarian.

In addition, when buying shares, Larisa considers it important to pay attention to the dividend yield. And buy when it becomes double-digit, i.e. when the stock price falls.

Well, we can only follow its example and include such companies in our own the dividend portfolio.
